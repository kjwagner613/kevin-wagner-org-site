Count 2 and Response

Menacing constituting Domestic Violence (ORS 163.190)

Statutory language

"A person commits the crime of menacing if by word or conduct the person intentionally attempts to place another person in fear of imminent serious physical injury."

DV inclusion note

When menacing constitutes domestic violence under ORS 132.586, the judgment reflects that designation. This may affect penalties, reporting, restraining orders, custody, and immigration consequences.

Qualification checklist

  • If the person acted intentionally, continue; otherwise not menacing (intent required).
  • If the person used words or conduct, continue; otherwise not menacing (no act).
  • If the act was intended to place another in fear, continue; otherwise not menacing.
  • If the feared harm is serious physical injury, continue; otherwise not menacing.
  • If the threatened harm was imminent, continue; otherwise not menacing.
  • If conduct was a substantial step toward causing that fear, menacing under ORS 163.190; otherwise not menacing.

Oregon definition of "imminent"

In Oregon cases, "imminent" means harm that is immediate and impending, not remote, speculative, or dependent on future events.

Response

This incident does not meet the qualifications or definitions required to proceed.
