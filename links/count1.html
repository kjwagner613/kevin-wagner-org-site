Count 1 and Response

Strangulation constituting Domestic Violence, a class C Felony

To meet the requirements of Class C felony, one of these standards must be met

  • The crime is committed in the immediate presence of, or is witnessed by, the person’s or the victim’s minor child or stepchild, or a minor child residing in the household of either party. 
  • Victim is under age 10
  • Victim is family member or household member as defined in ORS 135.230
  • During commission, person used, attempted to use, or threatened to use a dangerous or deadly weapon. 
  • The person has a **prior conviction** for this section or related assault offenses (ORS 163.160, 163.165, 163.175, 163.185, 163.190), and the **same victim** is involved. 
  • The person has **at least three previous convictions** for this section or equivalent assault crimes
  • The person commits the strangulation knowing the victim is pregnant

Oregon defines family or household member as:

  1. Spouse
  2. Former Spouse
  3. Adult related by blood or marriage
  4. Person cohabitating with each other (living together in a relationship similar to a spousal relationship)
  5. Persons who have cohabitated or been in a sexually intimate relationship
  6. Unmarried parents of a minor child
  7. None of these apply

How Oregon defines as Cohabitating.

  1. Key factors courts consider include:
  2. Nature of the relationship, not just duration
  3. Whether the living arrangement resembled a marriage-like partnership
  4. Shared residence and mutual responsibilities
  5. Presentation as a couple to others 
  6. None of these apply
